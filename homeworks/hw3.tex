\begin{hwexercise} Let $\phi \colon F \to G$ be a morphism of sheaves. Show that if $\phi_x \colon F_x \to G_x$ is injective for all $x$, then $\phi_U \colon F(U) \to G(U)$ is injective for all $U$. (That is, a monomorphism of sheaves is also a monomorphism of presheaves).
\end{hwexercise}


\begin{hwexercise} Write a quick argument that \v{C}ech cohomology is functorial in the sheaf, that is,
\begin{align*}
    \check{H}^q(X,-) \colon \Ab(X) \to \Ab
\end{align*}
is a functor for any $q\ge0$.
\end{hwexercise}

\begin{hwexercise} Verify some of the claims from class about this chain complex of sheaves $\Z_{\mathcal{U},\bullet}$ we defined:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item $\Z_{\mathcal{U},p}$ corepresents the functor $\check{C}^p(\mathcal{U},-)$
    \item the differentials on $\Z_{\mathcal{U},\bullet}$ induce the \v{C}ech differentials under the natural isomorphism above
    \item the differentials on $\Z_{\mathcal{U},\bullet}$ make it an exact sequence of sheaves for $\bullet\ge1$.
\end{enumerate}
\end{hwexercise}

\begin{hwexercise} Let $X$ be a Hausdorff space. Prove that it is paracompact if and only if every open cover of $X$ admits a locally finite refinement which has an associated partition of unity. (you may use the shrinking lemma and Urysohn's lemma)
\end{hwexercise}
